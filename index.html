Do I need to file taxes in India if I am an NRI?

If you are an NRI with income in India—like rent from property, interest on bank accounts or fixed deposits, or earnings from investments—you might need to file a tax return. Filing is mandatory if your taxable income in India exceeds threshold limit in a financial year.

PAN Card: To file returns and track taxes paid.

Bank Statements: For income or interest earned in India.

Investment Details: Like mutual funds, stocks, or property sale documents.

Passport: To verify your residential status (number of days in/out of India).

Form 16A: If TDS has been deducted from your income.

Additional documents like property agreements or Form 26AS (tax credit statement) might also be needed.

Yes, you can file your NRI tax return online using the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal. However, tax laws for NRIs can be complex, especially if you claim benefits under the DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement). Consulting a tax expert can help you avoid errors, maximize deductions, and save time.

The deadline for filing your Indian income tax return is July 31st of the assessment year. For example, for income earned in FY 2024-25, the filing deadline is July 31, 2025. Missing the deadline could lead to penalties or loss of benefits like carry-forward of losses.
